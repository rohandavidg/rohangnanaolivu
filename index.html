About

Data science at the frontier of medicine

Principal Bioinformatician · Mayo Clinic, Rochester MN

I've spent over a decade applying machine learning, deep learning, and large language models to some of the hardest problems in medicine — identifying causal genes in rare disease patients, classifying cancer-driving mutations, and predicting drug response from multi-omics data. My work sits at the intersection of rigorous statistical modeling and production AI engineering: I've shipped GCP-deployed genomic applications used in clinical workflows, built knowledge graph frameworks that improve diagnostic accuracy, and led multi-cohort studies spanning tens of thousands of patients. As Python steward for Mayo Clinic's Quantitative Health Sciences department, I also care deeply about code quality, reproducibility, and mentoring the next generation of computational scientists.

AI & Machine Learning

PyTorch, XGBoost, scikit-learn, deep neural networks, variational autoencoders, few-shot learning, and SHAP-based explainability across clinical prediction tasks.

Large Language Models

Gemini, LLaMA 3.1, Qwen2.5, Mistral on vLLM — prompt engineering, benchmarking, and clinical information extraction from unstructured notes.

Cloud & Engineering

GCP (Vertex AI, Cloud Run, BigQuery), Streamlit, Docker, Nextflow/WDL, SLURM HPC — from prototype to production deployment.

Genomics & Multi-Omics

NGS analysis, spatial transcriptomics, single-cell, protein language models (ProtTrans, AlphaFold2), knowledge graphs, and multi-cohort data integration.
